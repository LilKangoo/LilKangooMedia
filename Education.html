Welcome to our website, where we aim to provide you with the highest quality information on all things photography. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned pro we've got you covered with articles on a wide range of topics.

In the upcoming articles we'll dive into the importance of the rule of thirds, share composition techniques to elevate your photos & teach you how to capture action in all its glory. We'll also cover shooting in low light, creating depth in your photos & choosing the right lens for the job.

But it doesn't stop there. We'll also provide tips on portrait, landscape, street, food, product & macro photography as well as delve into the art of black & white and night photography. Plus, we'll share our expertise on event, architectural, fine art, fashion, and documentary photography.

But that's just the beginning. We'll also cover specialised areas such as wildlife, landscape portrait, still life, sports, travel, environmental portrait, and photojournalism. And for the more adventurous, we'll even cover underwater, pet, baby, senior portrait, wedding, family, glamour, advertising, industrial, nature, landscape astrophotography, concerts and music, theatrical, abstract, panoramic, intimate, equine, pet portrait, boudoir, maternity, newborn, lifestyle & corporate headshot photography.

So whether you're just starting out on your photography journey or looking to take your skills to the next level be sure to stay tuned for our high-quality articles on all of these exciting subjects.

Photography and videography individual classes available now.

Step 1: Introduction

Week 1

- Introduction to the world of photography, including the history and evolution of photography.
- Different types of cameras and lenses available, and their various uses.
- Basics of exposure, shutter speed, aperture, and ISO.
- Basics of composition and lighting.

Step 2: Understanding Light

Week 2

- How light affects the quality of a photograph and ways to use natural light, flash, and studio lighting.
- How light creates mood and atmosphere in a photo.
- Measuring light using light meters and adjusting exposure accordingly.

Step 3: Composition and Framing

Week 3

- Composition principles, including the rule of thirds, leading lines, and negative space.
- How to use cropping and framing to enhance photo impact.
- Examples of successful compositions.

Step 4: Shooting Techniques

Week 4

- Different shooting techniques, including panning, shooting from different angles, and capturing action.
- Manual focus and manual exposure settings.
- Different shooting modes, such as continuous shooting and burst mode.

Step 5: Editing and Post-Processing

Week 5

- Photo editing basics, including adjusting exposure, colour, contrast, and saturation.
- Editing software, such as Lightroom and Photoshop.
- How to correct common flaws, such as red-eye, lens distortion, and noise.

Step 6: Review and Portfolio

Week 6 - 8

- Reviewing students' photos and providing feedback.
- Students to create a portfolio of their best shots.
- Marketing and promoting your work through social media, online portfolios, and photography competitions.
- End the course with a final project where students create a series of photographs on a specific theme or subject.
