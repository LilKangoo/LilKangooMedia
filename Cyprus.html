Adventure Awaits:
Picture-Perfect Reasons to Visit This Island

By Michael Ben Gour

A trip to Cyprus is an adventure like no other! This beautiful Mediterranean island is full of stunning views and landscapes, making it an ideal destination for photography, videography and travel. With its rich cultural heritage, Cyprus provides an unforgettable experience that will stay with you long after your trip has ended. So why wait? Pack your bags and get ready for a picture-perfect journey - Cyprus awaits!

The Beaches
Cyprus is home to some of the most stunning beaches in the world from the warm crystal-clear waters of Limassol to the picturesque shores of Paphos. With its beautiful shorelines, breathtaking bays & secluded coves, Cyprus has something to offer for everyone. One of the most popular destinations is the Blue Lagoon in Paphos, where you can take a dip in the refreshing waters & take in the stunning views.

If you're looking for a truly unique experience you should head to Cape Greco National Park, where you can explore the underwater life and visit some of the caves. You can swim with exotic fish & even find dolphins and turtles! There are also plenty of activities available such as snorkeling, kayaking & sailing.
No matter where you go in Cyprus, one thing is certain: you'll never be too far from a beach! In fact, fun fact: there are over 1,500 kilometers (900 miles) of beaches in Cyprus. That's enough for everyone!


The Venues

When visiting Cyprus, you have an array of wonderful hotels to choose from. In Paphos, the five-star St. Raphael Resort is an excellent choice. With its own private beach, indoor and outdoor pools, and luxurious amenities, you'll be living like royalty. Limassol has the InterContinental Aphrodite Hills, which features a golf course and spa for complete relaxation. In Larnaca, the Mediterranean Beach Hotel is close to the harbour and has some of the most breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea.

open-air If you're looking for something livelier, Ayia Napa is the place for you. Home to some of the best nightlife in Europe, you can explore a variety of bars, clubs, and lounges. A fun fact about Cyprus night life is that it's one of the few places in the world where you can still experience true open air clubbing. Popular venues include the One Club, Dreams Beach Bar, and Cubana Lounge.
No trip to Cyprus would be complete without enjoying its vibrant nightlife. So take a break and hit the clubs for an unforgettable experience!

The Views

One popular spot for stunning views is Ayia Napa. This beautiful seaside town offers miles of white sand beaches, perfect for relaxing & taking in the beauty of the Mediterranean. There are also plenty of interesting activities to explore in Ayia Napa such as sightseeing tours, shopping & incredible cuisine.
Did you know that the waters surrounding Cyprus are the cleanest in Europe? This is due to the fact that there are no heavy industries around the island so it remains largely unspoiled by pollution. That makes it a great place for swimming, diving, and snorkeling!

Last but not least if you’re looking for a laugh, why not visit the ‘Funny Land’ theme park in Ayia Napa? With over 30 rides & attractions, this is one fun-filled day out you won’t want to miss!
So why not come to Cyprus and enjoy its breathtaking views? With so much to see and do, you’ll be sure to have an unforgettable experience. Just don't forget your camera or hire LilKangooMedia to capture your holiday on film!

Larnaca

Larnaca is a city on the south east coast of Cyprus and the capital of the district of the same name.

It is the third-largest city in the country, after Nicosia and Limassol, with a metro population of 144,200 in 2015

ABOUT
Larnaca...

Larnaca, a port town on the southeastern coast of Cyprus. The modern town, on the bay between Capes Kiti and Pyla, overlays much of ancient Citium, founded by the Mycenaeans in the 13th century BCE; it was rebuilt by the Byzantines. Citium was the birthplace of the Greek philosopher Zeno of Citium, who founded Stoicism. Its modern name (meaning “Funerary Urn” in Greek) recalls numerous tombs under its soil.
 
The modern port was developed during Ottoman occupation (1571–1878), after which it was enlarged after Turkish intervention closed northern Cyprus’ main port at Famagusta. Larnaca’s port accommodates small craft at dockside; larger ships anchor in the roadstead and are served by lighters (barges) or use two floating pontoons completed in 1977.

Potatoes and cement are exported through Larnaca.The town of Larnaca includes a tannery, an oil refinery, a cooperative cheese factory, and the production of commercial salt and brooms. Roads connect the town with Dhekelia, Pano Kophinou, and Mazotos. Larnaca International Airport has expanded since its opening as a temporary facility in 1974 after the closing of the international airport in Nicosia.

The surrounding region is largely agricultural. Crops include wheat, barley, potatoes, fruits, vegetables, and nuts. Industry is based mainly on agricultural produce and includes milled flour and canned fruit and vegetables. 

Salt is extracted from salt mines in the area to the west; copper and gypsum are mined at Kalavasos to the west as well. Population (2001) 70,502.

Paphos

Paphos - a city in the Republic of Cyprus, in the Paphos District. In 2011, it had a population of 32,892.

It is situated on the southwestern coast of Cyprus. An important tourist center. The country's fourth largest city.

ABOUT
Paphos...

Located on the southwest coast of Cyprus, Paphos (Pafos) offers a healthy lifestyle & one of the best climates in the Mediterranean.

Paphos (Pafos) is ideal for basking in the sunshine all year round & experiencing its hospitality wherever you go.

Plunge into the sparkling warm waters of the Mediterranean in summer, or venture out on a splendid nature trail and observe fields blanketed in wildflowers in spring; visit ancient and historical landmarks in autumn, and keep fit with a plethora of outdoor activities in winter. Whichever way you look at it, Paphos (Pafos) is a year-round destination with many reasons to visit!

Ayia Napa

Ayia Napa is a city in the Famagusta district of Cyprus. It has a population of 3,212 according to 2011 census data.

To the east of the village is Kawo Greko promontory, which is the most southeastern part of Cyprus. It is protected as Kavo Gkreko National Forest Park and Natura 2000 area.

ABOUT
Ayia Napa...

The east of Cyprus, including the resort town of Ayia Napa and its neighboring villages, is renowned for its beaches. Since the 1990s, it has been one of the Mediterranean's most popular party destinations, attracting crowds of young clubbers from across Europe every year. The area's rocky coastline is dotted with sandy shores in little coves offering a choice of great places to swim and sunbathe. The top picks include Nissi Beach & Makronissos Beach.

Ayia Napa has much more to offer than only its hedonistic summertime vibe despite being recognized for it. The Blue Lagoon, the Monastery of Ayia Napa & the National Forest Park of Cape Greco which are all included on the UNESCO World Heritage List are just a few of the other impressive historical & natural landmarks in the area. There are also several nearby water parks and amusement parks. Watch out for the fantastic water parks and amusement parks if you're traveling with a family.
